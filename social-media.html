SOCIAL MEDIA

What is my specific objection to social media?  In a nutshell, I believe that it does far more harm than good, and has contributed materially to many problems we face today.

With the sole exception of LinkedIn, my observations about that social media include:

  • Highly addictive, especially to children and teenagers
  • Engineered to maximize that addiction, known in the industry as "engagement"
  • Inconsistent with personal values, including placing profit before principle
  • Controlled by people of dubious character, which is why profits come before principle and little is done to fix
  • Manipulated by governments
  • Used as mass-population surveillance apparatus by governments
  • Used to surveil individuals and organizations deemed undesirable by governments
  • Pooling the most private data imaginable about people
  • Contributing to the spread of anti-semitism and other hate-filled ideas
  • Echo chambers for political belief that result in further division and distrust
  • Enshittification
  • Mass-scale mood and sentiment manipulation experiments conducted quietly
  • Negative impact on the mental health of teenagers, especially teenage girls
  • Mob justice / doxxing / destroying people for sport
  • Influencer nonsense
  • Pig butchering scams aplenty
  • Erosion of democracy - January 6th would be one very obvious example
  • Clickbait economics motivating sensationalism 
  • Fake news spreads unchecked and faster than real news
  • Angry reactions are rewarded and amplified by algorithms tuned to maximize "engagement" - a bit like adding ammonia to tobacco to make cigarettes even more addictive than they naturally otherwise would be 
  • A shocking bad signal-to-noise ratio
  • Catering to advertisers instead of the end users
  • "If you aren't paying for it, you are the product"

So what makes LinkedIn different?  Why is LinkedIn OK, but not Facebook?

  • Signal-to-noise ratio is much higher
  • Attracts a different audience that tends to be more open-minded and tolerant
  • Actually helps people find jobs, learn and further their careers
  • Useful, unique user-generated content that is not clickbait
  • Very smart people from across disciplines, countries, faiths, background, etc. 
  • A general willingness (by users) to debate topics and hear the other side without decrying anyone you disagree with a heathen or heretic or evil person -- or sicking the mob of blind followers on them to intimidate and harass.  There are definitely exceptions to this but they are relatively few and far between.  
  • Downside:  Only trivially-better handling of bad actors and obvious scams.  This is an area LinkedIn needs to radically rethink and improve.  As but one example, they do not take pig butchering scam-spam seriously, even when it is very obvious.  Another issue is that they make it far too easy for scammers to create false accounts and large networks of connections, making them look legitimate to the untrained eye.
